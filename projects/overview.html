My research explores the basic computational principles in creative problem-solving and social reasoning. The research is highly interdisciplinary, linking Computer Science with Philosophy, Psychology and Neuroscience.

How can robots do creative problem solving while acting within society's normative constraints? For example, how can a robot reason that sometimes a coin can be used as a screwdriver but not always, like when the coin in question is someone else's rare and precious collectable?

For my dissertation, I am developing computational methods focused on three areas that map to core abilities required for practical creativity:

  1. Perceiving Affordances: Reasoning about functionality of objects in different social contexts. What can and should one do with objects around them?
  2. Creative Exploration: How can one extend their knowledge and discover a new action capability, a new concept, or a new use for an object through environmental exploration and play?
  3. Handling Uncertainty: How can one reason and make decisions when the information available is uncertain and in short supply?

Underlying these areas is a hidden but a equally interesting 4th question:

  1. Machine Ethics: What are the legal, ethical and moral implications of artificial creative intelligence? Can we use computational methods to ensure robots learn and abide by norms?
