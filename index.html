I am a PhD student in Computer Science and Cognitive Science at Tufts University. My research is in Artificial Intelligence, Robotics and Machine Ethics. What cognitive abilities make us clever, resourceful and creative problem solvers? Should we endow robots with these abilities? If so, how?

I also enjoy drawing cartoons. Some of my work can be seen on Legally Drawn, cartoons poking fun at law and lawyers.

Background 

I grew up in Muscat, Oman and moved to the United States for college. After graduating summa cum laude in Electrical Engineering from the University of Arkansas in 2003, I studied at MIT, where I conducted research in the mathematical modeling of printed circuit board insulation at the Laboratory for Electromagnetic and Electronic Systems (LEES @ RLE). I received a Masters Degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from MIT in 2005. From 2005 to 2013, I worked as an Intellectual Property Lawyer at Ropes & Gray, during which time I concurrently obtained a JD from Boston University School of Law. My love for science, math, the human thought process and creativity pulled me back into the world of scientific research.
